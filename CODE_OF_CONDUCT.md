# Code of Conduct

## Standard

This project adopts the [Contributor Covenant v2.1](https://www.contributor-covenant.org/version/2/1/code_of_conduct/)
without modification. The full text is authoritative and governs
participation in issues, pull requests, discussions, and any other
project space (public or private).

## Summary for first-time contributors

Be kind. Assume good faith. Give and receive criticism about the work,
not the person making the work. Historical subject matter — conquest,
genocide, displacement, contested identities — is heavy, and honest
disagreement about it is expected. Ad-hominem attacks, slurs,
harassment, and dismissiveness toward under-represented histories are
not.

## Academic-integrity addendum

Because AtlasPI publishes historical claims that will be read and
reused, this project also expects:

- **Claim your data.** Do not submit content you cannot source or stand
  behind. Fabricated or LLM-hallucinated citations are treated as a
  serious conduct issue, not merely a quality issue.
- **Disagree with evidence.** When challenging an existing entity,
  boundary, or ETHICS decision, cite — either a primary source or a
  specific academic work. "This looks wrong" without evidence is not a
  productive contribution.
- **Respect contested histories.** For ethnic, national, religious, and
  colonial disputes, expect us to preserve multiple views
  simultaneously (see ETHICS-003). Refusing to let one view be recorded
  alongside yours is not compatible with the project.

## Reporting

Conduct issues can be reported privately to **clirim@cra-srl.com**.
All reports are read by the maintainer in confidence. Retaliation
against a good-faith reporter is itself a conduct violation.

## Enforcement

The Contributor Covenant v2.1 enforcement ladder applies: private
warning → public warning → temporary ban → permanent ban. Decisions
are the maintainer's and are final for the project, though they can
be re-evaluated on appeal to `clirim@cra-srl.com` no sooner than 30
days after the action.
